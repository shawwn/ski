Rates of Change

Over the course of this article, we'll be developing a geometric intuition for rates of change, when a quantity changes over time. In other words, we'll be rediscovering Calculus. We'll assume some basic knowledge of mathematics: you know what an equation is, and how to do arithmetic. But beyond that, you won't need much else.

Let's say we're painting the outside wall of our house. We want it a lovely shade of magenta, since we enjoy incurring massive HOA fines. We start painting in the middle, and after a few strokes we have a magenta rectangle:
